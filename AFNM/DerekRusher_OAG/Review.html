Informal Review
Leonardo da Vinci has created a masterpiece with the "Mona Lisa". The way he conveys emotion through her half-smile and staring eyes is amazing. He really brings the foreground to life and puts the perfect amount of empasis on it.. Take a look back at the painting. What is the first thing you look at? Chances are, it's her face, and more specifically her eyes, which will stare back at you. The background is impressive as well, the winding path and serene outdoors by the river give a sense of peace, combined with the muddled colors not only in the background but in the whole painting to create a nice feeling for all those who look at her. Da Vinci's use of cooler colors, specifically dark blues with a few greens and purples is perfectly contrasted by the yellow of the Lisa herself. Da Vinci does this so subtly, it's is arguably the most impressive part of the painting. Da Vinci knows how to take an image of peson and bring it to life through his use of contrasting and muddling color, and his ability to mold the faces of his subjects into something incredibly unique and indescribable. The "Mona Lisa" is a painting that simply jumps out at you. Her face is in your face the second you look at the painting, and the myth that her eyes follow you everywhere you go is not a myth. The colors in the painting itself give off such a calming and relaxing feeling, putting the same nonchalant, yet indescribable expression the Lisa wears on your face. Possibly even more intriguing than the painting is the mystery behind it. The Mona Lisa translates to "My Lady" in the in the Italian Leonardo da Vinci would have used, so noone knew who the lady in the painting was. Some said it was Princess Isabella, others said it was just someone da Vinci randomly met, still othes claimed it was the artist's mother, and it was even proposed that Salai, da Vinci's apprentice, was dressed in women's clothes for the part. Perhaps the most likely answer is Lisa del Giocondo, the wife of a rich Florentine silk merchant, as this is what one early biographer claimed, but also because the orrigional Italian name for the painting was "La Giocondo". In any case, this painting is truly a world treasure.
